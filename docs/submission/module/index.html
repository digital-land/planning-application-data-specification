Submission modules

Modules available for submission applications.

Module Description
access-rights-of-way Details of any changes the proposed development would make to existing access arrangements or public rights of way
adj-premises Details of properties next to the development site
advert-location Where the advertisement being applied to be built will be located
advert-period How long the proposed advertisement will be shown.
advertisement-types What type of advertisements are proposed and how many there will be.
agent-contact Name and contact information if an agent is being used.
agent-details Name and contact information if an agent is being used.
applicant-contact Telephone number and email address of the applicant.
applicant-details Name and contact information for the parties making the application.
bio-geo-arch-con Details of potential impacts to the biodiversity of the site, or any noteable archaeological or geological features.
bng How any natural habitats on the development site will be improved by the proposed works.
checklist Checking whether all the requirements of the form have been met, such as proof of payment or supporting documentation.
community-consultation What community consultation activities have taken place as part of the application
con-remove-vary Why the applicant is asking for planning conditions to be removed or changed.
conflict-of-interest Details of any conflict of interest that may exist between the applicant and planning authority.
declaration Signed and dated verification of the application's accuracy.
demolition Details of any demolition that needs to take place as part of the development proposal.
demolition-reason Why demolition is necessary at the development site
desc-existing-use How the development site is used, including use class information
desc-proposed-works Details of development plans such as extensions measurements or work specifications
desc-proposed-works-lb-ldc Details of development plans for the work to the listed building an applicant is seeking a lawful development certificate for
desc-work-impacts-risks How the proposed development may affect nearby amenities, air traffic, defence assets or protected views.
desc-your-proposal Written description of the proposed development including any additional relevant details.
designated-areas Details of any 'designated area' the develpoment site is on, such as a Conservation Area or National Park.
dev-type Supporting information for developments used for oil and gas exploration or mining
discharge-con How any conditions imposed as part of being given planning permission will be met
eligibility Whether certain eligibility criteria has been met and the right people notified
eligibility-current-building How the current building meets eligibity criteria
eligibility-extension How a proposal to build an extension meets relevant criteria.
eligibility-proposal How the proposed development meets eligibility criteria
eligibility-related-works Whether any related works such as scaffolding required will affect the eligibility of the planning proposal
employment How the proposed development will impact existing and proposed employee numbers
equip-method How oil and gas will be extracted as part of the proposed development.
existing-use How the site is currently being used.
flood-risk-assessment Results of any flood risk assessments made for the development site
foul-sewage How waste water will leave the property as part of the proposed development
grounds-existing-use Supporting inforation for a Lawful Development Certificate application relating to how the site has most recently been used.
grounds-for-application Why a Certificate of Lawfulness of Propose Works is being requested.
grounds-ldc Evidence and explanations relating to a Lawful Development Certificate (LDC) application
grounds-proposed-use What the new site will be used for
haz-substances Details of hazardous substances requiring consent used as part of the development
hedgerow-removal Details of any hedgerows being removed as part of the development
hrs-operation Proposed operating hours if the proposed development is intended for non-residential use.
immunity-from-listing Whether the applicant has obtained a Certificate of Immunity (COI) meaning the building in question cannot be listed
info-support-ldc Supporting information required to make a Lawful Development Certificate application
interest-details Names and contact details for all parties with an interest in the proposed develpoment.
interest-in-land Whether the applicant owns or has permission to use the land where the proposed advertisement will be placed
lb-alter Details of any changes being made to a listed building as part of development works
lb-grade The grade of any listed building affected by the proposed development.
ldc-interest Who had a say in whether the proposal should go ahead
materials What materials are being used for the proposed development
nm-amendment-details Details of changes being requested to plans after permission has already been granted.
non-res-floorspace Details of changes to non-residential floorspace in the proposed development.
oilgas-permission-type Module for details about types of onshore oil and gas extraction permissions already received and applying for
ownership-certs Who will be affected by the proposal and whether they have been notified, such as agricultural tenants
parking Details of any changes the proposed development would make to parking facilities.
part-discharge Details of how the applicant is meeting a specific part of a set of conditions made by the planning authority.
plans-drawings-supporting-materials Additional materials and specifications that form part of the planning application
pre-app-advice Details of pre-application advice previously received from the planning authority
processes-machinery-waste How waste will be managed on the site
processes-machinery-waste-outline If making an 'outline' application, how waste will be managed on the development site
proposal-details What development, works or change of use is proposed
proposal-details-inc-non-residential Details of the residential and non-residential parts of the proposed development.
proposal-details-ldc Details of why a Lawful Development Certificate is required.
proposed-advert-details Details of the proposed advertisements such as their size and how they are made
related-applications Details of any other development proposals made for the site
res-units Details of the residential units that make up both the current and proposed development.
site-area How big the site is including relevant measurements
site-details Where the proposed development will be built.
site-info Any additional relevant information about the development site.
site-ownership For oil and gas extraction developments, who owns or has an interest in the site.
site-visit Information to help the planning authority arrange a site visit
storage-facilities For oil and gas extraction developments, how chemicals will be stored
supporting-info Any additional information which will help with the planning application
tpo Details of any Tree Preservation Orders (TPO) affecting the development site
trade-effluent Details of any liquid waste produced by industial processes on the proposed site, and how it will be diposed of.
tree-work-details Details of trees affected by the proposed development and what work is being done to them.
trees-additional Further details of any issues relating to trees on the site
trees-hedges Details of trees and/or hedges that will be affected by the proposed development
trees-location Where trees affected by the proposed development are located.
trees-ownership Who owns any trees affected by the proposed development.
use-works-activity Why a Lawful Development Certificate is required regarding how the development site is being used, or specific works taking place on the site.
vehicle-parking Details of current parking facilities at the site and any changes that would be made by the proposed development.
vol-agreement Details of any voluntary agreements made as part of an oil and gas extraction application.
waste-storage-collection Any waste storage or recycling arrangements are in place, such as waste storage areas
